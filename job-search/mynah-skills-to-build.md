# Mynah Skills to Build for Oanh's Job Search / AI Builder Work

_Last updated: 2026-05-21_

## Highest-value skills

### 1. Recruiter / HR screen skill

Purpose: evaluate jobs the way a recruiter or hiring manager might screen Oanh's profile.

Should cover:
- Must-have vs nice-to-have requirements.
- Likely ATS/recruiter screen risks.
- How to translate Oanh's nontraditional AI builder profile into role language.
- Whether to apply, network first, or skip.
- What evidence to foreground: Google/McKinsey/Intuit, AI portfolio, Paper & Pixel, StencilCV, Plinthica, etc.

### 2. Hiring-manager narrative skill

Purpose: shape Oanh's story for specific roles and interviews.

Should cover:
- Why this pivot.
- Why not traditional strategy/ops anymore.
- Why she is credible despite not being a software engineer.
- How her AI apps show product judgment, user empathy, and execution.
- Role-specific positioning for EdTech, health, public sector, agentic workflows, and AI enablement.

### 3. Job discovery / freshness skill

Purpose: find 3-5 good opportunities twice a week without relying only on stale LinkedIn listings.

Should cover:
- Search sources: company career pages, Google jobs, Wellfound, VC portfolio job boards, GovTech/MOE/MOHT, university/learning orgs, AI startup lists, recruiter posts, LinkedIn only as one signal.
- Freshness checks: posting date, company page confirmation, role repost patterns.
- Mix: actual applications + outreach/networking leads.
- Avoid overloading Oanh.

### 4. Portfolio packaging skill

Purpose: turn Oanh's apps into credible artifacts for hiring and outreach.

Should cover:
- One-page portfolio refinement.
- Product case studies.
- Public/private proof boundaries.
- Screenshots/demo language.
- How to explain non-code AI building without underselling it.

### 5. Outreach skill

Purpose: draft concise, human outreach that does not feel needy or generic.

Should cover:
- Hiring manager messages.
- Warm-network asks.
- Recruiter messages.
- Portfolio-led outreach.
- Follow-up cadence.

### 6. Non-technical AI enablement business skill

Purpose: help Oanh test the idea of helping non-technical people operationalize AI.

Should cover:
- Offer design.
- 1:1 and small-group formats.
- Pilot scripts.
- Case study templates.
- Pricing experiments.
- Segment selection: small businesses, parents, professionals, job seekers.
