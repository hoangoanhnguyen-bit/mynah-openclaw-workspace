## 1. StencilCV / Reso — AI career application engine

**Problem**  
Job applications are painful, repetitive, and strategically unclear. Most people do not know how to translate their actual experience into a role-specific narrative.

**User**  
Mid-career professionals and career switchers applying to roles where positioning matters.

**What AI does**  
- Sharpens a master profile CV.
- Analyzes job descriptions.
- Identifies gaps and application strategy.
- Generates tailored CVs and cover letters.
- Supports resume updating and interview prep through a chatbot.

**What I built**  
An end-to-end AI-assisted job application workflow designed around one master profile rather than many static résumé variants.

**Why it matters**  
Turns AI from a generic writing assistant into a structured career strategy and application system.

**Status / proof**  
Private/product-in-progress; actively dogfooding for my own job search.

## 2. Ink Radicals — Chinese character learning through components

**Problem**  
Chinese character learning often defaults to rote memorization, making it difficult for learners to understand structure, meaning, and recall cues.

**User**  
Chinese language learners, especially students who benefit from tactile/visual learning.

**What AI does**  
Supports explanation, learning flow, and potentially adaptive hints around semantic and phonetic components.

**What I built**  
A learning tool based on manipulating character components, designed around the pedagogical principle that learners retain characters better when they understand semantic and phonetic structure.

**Why it matters**  
Demonstrates AI-assisted learning design grounded in pedagogy, language structure, and learner experience.

**Status / proof**  
Public Paper & Pixel product.

## 4. Plinthica — competitions discovery + verification agents

**Problem**  
College admissions and enrichment ecosystems are expensive, opaque, and stressful. Even paid consultants cannot know every credible competition or opportunity.

**User**  
Students and families looking for accurate information about competitions, enrichment opportunities, deadlines, eligibility, and fit.

**What AI does**  
- Discovers competitions and opportunities.
- Verifies information from source materials.
- Helps structure fit, credibility, deadlines, and requirements.

**What I built**  
An AI-assisted competition intelligence system starting with discovery and verification, rather than pretending to be an omniscient admissions strategist.

**Why it matters**  
Reduces information asymmetry in a high-pressure, high-cost industry without pushing families into over-optimized coaching.

**Status / proof**  
In development.

## 6. AI-assisted learning games — child-led co-creation

**Products / prototypes**

- **Fruit Boomerang** — throw boomerangs to clear bouncing fruit; originated from a child’s spoken idea.
- **Balloon Pop** — color discrimination / reflex game designed by a 5-year-old.
- **Letterbug** — AI-powered word game for vocabulary and word formation.
- **AquaPath** — physics/logic puzzle where players draw a path for water to flow.
- **Math is Fun!** — arithmetic practice with adjustable challenge levels.
- **Paper Games** — printable word searches, mazes, crosswords, and screen-free activities.

**Problem**  
Children often consume digital games rather than create them. Parents may want creative AI learning experiences but lack coding confidence.

**User**  
Families, children, and parents exploring AI-assisted creation.

**What AI does**  
Turns child ideas and parent prompts into playable prototypes; supports rapid iteration and creative exploration.

**What I built**  
A family of AI-assisted learning and play prototypes, many co-designed with children, exploring how AI can translate imagination into playable systems.

**Why it matters**  
Shows AI as a tool for agency, creativity, logic, and learning — not just content generation.

**Status / proof**  
Public games site under Paper & Pixel.
