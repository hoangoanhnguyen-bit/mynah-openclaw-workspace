# Oanh Job Search Strategy

_Last updated: 2026-05-21_

## Core diagnosis

This is not a normal job search. Oanh is trying to move away from strategy/ops/chief-of-staff/product-ops work that she can do but no longer cares about, toward work that uses the creative energy she has found building AI-native apps.

Financial context matters: Oanh and Dang have built a sizeable net worth (~$4.2M USD), likely near or at financial independence. That means the search should not optimize only for salary/status, but Google compensation still matters if staying at Google.

## Emerging positioning

**AI-native builder/operator focused on learning, creativity, public-good products, and high-stakes personal decision tools.**

Shorter version:

> I build AI-native tools that help non-technical people learn, create, and make better high-stakes decisions.

Important narrative shift:

- Not: “senior ops person dabbling in AI.”
- Instead: “senior Google/McKinsey operator who has become a hands-on AI product builder, with portfolio proof.”

## Product thesis from portfolio

Oanh’s apps are converging around:

> AI tools for high-stakes personal decisions and learning contexts where existing expert-advice industries are expensive, opaque, uneven, or stressful.

Examples:

- StencilCV / Reso: job applications and career positioning.
- Plinthica: competitions discovery and verification for students/families.
- Paper & Pixel games/playbook: AI fluency and child-led creative learning.
- Ink Radicals: language learning through tactile component understanding.
- Earnings-Churnings: private trading decision support; do not publicize as financial product due to regulatory concerns.

Adjacent thesis:

> Helping non-technical people turn AI from a chat toy into working personal/business systems.

This should be framed as practical AI implementation/workflow design, not generic AI training. See `nontech-ai-enablement-business-thesis.md`.

## Target lanes

### 1. Best-fit jobs

AI product roles in education, health, public good, learning, creativity.

Examples:

- MOE / GovTech Senior AI Product Manager, Mother Tongue Languages.
- MOHT Product Manager — AI & Digital Mental Health.
- EdTech / learning product roles.
- Creative AI / interactive education product roles.

### 2. Strategic bridge jobs

AI transformation / agentic workflow / internal tools roles, only if:

- L6+ or equivalent seniority,
- strong team,
- strong compensation,
- not pure deck-making / stakeholder theater,
- gives escape from current team without sacrificing too much energy.

### 3. Portfolio/business path

Continue building Paper & Pixel / StencilCV / Plinthica. Use job search as product validation loop for StencilCV.

Explore a service-led lane helping non-technical people operationalize AI through 1:1 or small-group implementation sessions. This may be more natural than large workshops and could become portfolio proof, paid consulting, or productized playbooks.

## Job rubric

### Apply hard

- AI learning / EdTech product.
- AI health / regulated public-good product.
- Creative AI tools / interactive learning.
- Roles where portfolio proof matters.
- Roles with hands-on product building or close product leadership.

### Maybe / networking only

- Agentic AI transformation roles with real product/workflow design.
- Internal Google L6+ AI roles where team quality and comp are strong.
- Arts/culture tech roles if they involve product, digital learning, audience engagement, or creative technology.

### Skip / old-life trap

- Generic strategy & ops.
- Chief of staff / product ops unless survival move.
- AI adoption/change-management workshop roles.
- Cloud sales / GTM / account transformation roles unless comp/team are exceptional.
- Junior roles / downlevel roles. Oanh already downleveled once at Google and does not want to again.
