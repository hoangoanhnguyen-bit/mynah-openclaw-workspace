# Non-technical AI Enablement Thesis

_Last updated: 2026-05-21_

## Trigger

Oanh noted that she may have a lane helping non-technical people use AI for more than chatting. She tried a workshop for non-technical friends after taking AI/building courses taught by engineers and developers, but one large session did not work well: too much to cover, mixed AI familiarity, and large-group teaching is not her natural format. She is more comfortable with 1:1 or small-group help. She also recently helped an F&B business owner set up OpenClaw so the owner could have an assistant.

## Core insight

This is a real lane, but it probably should not be packaged as "AI training" or generic workshops.

Better positioning:

> Practical AI implementation partner for non-technical founders, parents, and professionals who want AI to do useful work in their actual life/business.

Even sharper:

> I help non-technical people turn AI from a chat toy into working personal/business systems.

## Why Oanh is credible

- She is non-technical by background, so she understands the intimidation/friction firsthand.
- She has crossed the gap herself: from strategy/operator to hands-on AI app builder.
- She has built concrete tools: StencilCV/Reso, Plinthica, Ink Radicals, ChromaClef, Paper & Pixel games/playbook.
- She can translate between user pain, workflow design, prompting, product structure, and practical implementation.
- Her portfolio is proof that she can make AI useful without presenting herself as a software engineer.

## Who this could serve

### 1. Non-technical solopreneurs / small business owners

Examples: F&B owner, coaches, tutors, consultants, creative businesses, small agencies.

Needs:
- Set up an AI assistant for repetitive admin.
- Turn messy business knowledge into reusable workflows.
- Draft replies, menus, listings, FAQs, client follow-ups, social posts.
- Organize docs, SOPs, customer notes, recurring tasks.
- Understand what is safe/not safe to automate.

Likely offer:
- 1:1 AI workflow setup session.
- Follow-up implementation sprint.
- Lightweight retainer/check-in.

### 2. Parents / families

Needs:
- Help kids use AI creatively rather than passively.
- Build simple games/projects together.
- Understand safe, age-appropriate use.
- Learn by doing without technical jargon.

Likely offer:
- Small-group family AI building labs.
- Parent-child 1:1 co-building sessions.
- Paper & Pixel playbook as entry product.

### 3. Mid-career professionals

Needs:
- Use AI for job search, positioning, writing, research, learning, personal workflows.
- Build small personal tools without becoming developers.
- Reduce fear of being left behind.

Likely offer:
- AI personal operating system setup.
- StencilCV-led job search workflow.
- Small cohort for "build one useful thing with AI."

## What probably will not work

- One-off broad workshops for mixed-skill audiences.
- Abstract AI literacy classes.
- Teaching coding or developer-style toolchains as the core promise.
- Positioning as "prompt engineering".
- Large-group formats before the content has been modularized and tested.

## Better format hypothesis

Start service-led, not course-led.

1. Pick one narrow user segment.
2. Offer a 1:1 or 2-4 person pilot.
3. Promise one concrete outcome, not general AI fluency.
4. Document the recurring pain points and templates.
5. Turn repeated pieces into playbooks/products later.

Examples:
- "In 90 minutes, we set up your AI business assistant for customer replies + weekly content drafts."
- "In 2 sessions, you and your child build a simple game from their idea."
- "In 2 hours, we build your AI job-search cockpit: master profile, role tracker, fit analysis, outreach drafts."

## Relationship to job search

This thesis strengthens Oanh's job-search story. It shows she is not merely trying to become a traditional PM or a technical builder. Her differentiated lane is:

> AI adoption through real products and workflows for non-technical users.

This can support roles in:
- AI learning / EdTech product.
- AI transformation / agentic workflows.
- Public-good digital products.
- Internal tools / enablement products.
- Founder/community/creator-facing AI products.

But she should avoid roles where "AI enablement" means mostly enterprise change management decks.
