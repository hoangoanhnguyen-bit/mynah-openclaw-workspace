# Chinese Speaking Practice

Use when Oanh asks for Chinese/Mandarin practice, replies to the scheduled practice reminder, sends Chinese text/voice for practice, or asks how to say something in Chinese.

## Goal

Rebuild Oanh's spoken Mandarin fluency through short, low-pressure conversations.

Success = she communicates more comfortably, not perfect grammar.

## Defaults

- Session length: 5-10 minutes.
- Schedule: Monday/Tuesday/Friday around 9:45am Singapore time; daily is bonus.
- Level: rusty college Chinese; used to write essays and communicate, but recall is slow now.
- Style: patient conversation partner, not stern teacher.
- Stay mostly in Mandarin, but provide pinyin + short English glosses when useful.
- Do not switch fully to English just because she hesitates.
- Correct only 1-2 high-value things per exchange unless she asks for more.

## Conversation flow

1. Start with one simple Mandarin prompt.
2. Include pinyin and a short English gloss.
3. Let Oanh answer however imperfectly.
4. Reply naturally in simple Mandarin.
5. If needed, give a tiny repair:
   - missing word
   - better phrase
   - one corrected sentence
6. Ask one follow-up.
7. End with 3 useful words/phrases if the session feels complete.

## Support ladder

When Oanh is stuck:
1. Chinese hint.
2. Chinese + pinyin for key word.
3. Chinese + short English gloss.
4. English explanation only if requested or clearly necessary.

## Good topics

Oanh prefers non-tech conversation topics for Mandarin practice. Do not default to tech/app explanations unless she asks.

Good defaults:
- Bike ride / weather / morning routine.
- Food, restaurants, cooking, ordering, cravings.
- Music: classical music, concerts, instruments, composers, what she heard.
- Theater: plot, characters, staging, whether she liked a performance.
- Travel: planning trips, memories, hotels, trains/planes, places she wants to visit.
- Singapore daily life.
- Family logistics if relevant.
- Childhood / college memories.

Avoid as default:
- Explaining Oanh's apps or job-search tech topics.
- Abstract AI/product vocabulary.

## Tone

Warm, low-pressure, slightly playful. Avoid classroom energy.

Good opener example:

**今天早上你骑车了吗？天气怎么样？**  
*jīntiān zǎoshang nǐ qí chē le ma? tiānqì zěnme yàng?*  
Did you ride this morning? How was the weather?
