# Job Application Narrative

Use when drafting LinkedIn copy, outreach, cover notes, interview answers, portfolio blurbs, or application strategy.

## Core narrative

Oanh's strongest frame:

> I build AI-native tools that help non-technical people learn, create, and make better high-stakes decisions.

Expanded:

> I combine senior operator judgment from Google, McKinsey, and Intuit with hands-on AI product building through Paper & Pixel. My work focuses on practical AI tools for learning, creativity, and high-stakes personal decisions.

## Avoid

- "Non-technical person dabbling in AI."
- Overclaiming as an engineer.
- Sounding like generic AI strategy/change management.
- Apologizing for an unconventional path.
- Too many products at once without a unifying thesis.

## Emphasize

- Operator judgment + product taste + user empathy.
- Hands-on AI building and dogfooding.
- Non-technical user insight as an advantage.
- Concrete apps/prototypes, not abstract AI enthusiasm.
- Ability to translate ambiguity into workflows, products, and adoption.

## Role-specific angles

- EdTech / learning: Ink Radicals, ChromaClef, Paper & Pixel, child-led games.
- Career / high-stakes decisions: StencilCV/Reso.
- Public good / health: regulated product judgment, safety/privacy/ethics, Google Play trusted experience.
- Agentic workflows: StencilCV, Plinthica, F&B owner OpenClaw setup, non-tech AI enablement thesis.
- Creative AI: Paper & Pixel games, playbook, parent-child creation.

## Standard outputs

For a target role, produce:
- 2-3 sentence positioning summary.
- Resume bullet angles.
- Portfolio proof points.
- Outreach note.
- Interview objections + answers.
- "Why this pivot" answer.

Keep drafts concise and human. No corporate sludge.
