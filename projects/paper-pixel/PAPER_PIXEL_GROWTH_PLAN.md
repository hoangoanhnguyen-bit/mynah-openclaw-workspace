## Core Positioning

Do not lead with "AI game building" as the main emotional hook.

Lead with:

> Turn passive screen time into creative confidence with your kid.

Supporting ideas:
- Parent as coach, not coder.
- Your kid learns to direct AI, not just consume AI.
- The game is the vehicle; the real product is creative confidence, clear communication, critical thinking, and AI fluency.
- This is for non-technical parents, homeschoolers, and teachers who want to prepare kids for AI without adding zombie screen time.

## Strongest Proof Assets

Use these repeatedly in copy and outreach:
- "No, I want to build the app with you."
- Letterbug was built in about an hour and approved for classroom use.
- AquaPath went from crayon sketch to playable game.
- Fruit Boomerang was spoken into existence by a 6-year-old.
- "There's no fruit in level 1" = a 6-year-old's first bug report.
- A parent does not need to code; they need to ask good questions and guide the process.

### 4. Weekly Growth Sprint

Goal: avoid a giant marketing backlog.

Each week:
1. Pick one channel.
2. Generate the assets.
3. Build the outreach list.
4. Send/post approved items.
5. Track replies, clicks, signups, and sales.
6. Decide next test.

Tasks I can do:
- Run the weekly review.
- Recommend the next test.
- Draft the week's content/outreach.
- Maintain the tracker.

Oanh's role:
- Approve the sprint direction.
- Send/post if the channel requires her identity or private account access.

### 5. Landing Page Optimizer

Goal: improve conversion from people who already click.

Tasks I can do:
