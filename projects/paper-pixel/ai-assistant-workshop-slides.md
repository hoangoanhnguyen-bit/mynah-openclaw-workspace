# Build a Personal AI Assistant Workflow

Draft Google Slides deck for Oanh / Paper & Pixel workshop.

## Slide 1 — Build a Personal AI Assistant Workflow
Subtitle: No coding required. Bring one recurring life-admin headache.
Notes: Frame this as practical and approachable, not a technical install workshop.

## Slide 2 — Why this workshop exists
- A real example: weekly fruit group-buy coordination
- Messy inputs: seller lists, group orders, payments, delivery constraints
- An app felt too heavy
- A personal AI assistant workflow felt just right
Notes: Tell the story lightly; it grounds the workshop.

## Slide 3 — The key idea
- You do not need to “build an app” for every problem
- Many problems need:
  - memory/context
  - careful parsing
  - a repeatable checklist
  - drafts/summaries
  - human approval
- That is exactly where AI assistants help

## Slide 4 — Bot vs app vs AI project
- App: buttons, database, fixed workflow
- Chatbot: answers questions
- AI project/assistant workflow: remembers context, handles messy inputs, produces structured outputs
- Today: we focus on Projects/Gems, not technical bot installs

## Slide 5 — What AI is good at
- Turning messy text into clean tables
- Extracting action items and deadlines
- Drafting messages in your tone
- Checking against rules/context
- Producing checklists and summaries

## Slide 6 — What AI should not do alone
- Send messages without review
- Make payments or financial decisions
- Assume ambiguous facts are true
- Handle private data casually
- Update important records without a human check

## Slide 7 — Pick your recurring pain
Ask yourself:
- What task repeats weekly/monthly?
- What information do I keep re-explaining?
- What mistakes are annoying or costly?
- What output would save me time?
- Where must I stay in control?

## Slide 8 — The workflow map
Input → Context → Rules → Output → Human approval
Example:
- Input: messy WhatsApp orders
- Context: names, prices, usual rules
- Rules: calculate totals, flag missing info
- Output: clean table + reminder draft
- Approval: human checks before sending/updating

## Slide 9 — Sample setup: group admin helper
Use case: WhatsApp group orders / RSVPs / volunteers
Project context:
- What the group is for
- Usual participants/categories
- Rules for totals, deadlines, reminders
Outputs:
- Clean order table
- Missing replies
- Draft reminder
- Final summary

## Slide 10 — Sample setup: school logistics helper
Use case: school emails, events, forms, costumes, payments
Project context:
- Child names/classes
- School routines
- Calendar preferences
Outputs:
- Action items
- Deadlines
- Calendar-ready text
- Parent checklist

## Slide 11 — Sample setup: payment reconciliation helper
Use case: group payments over days/weeks
Project context:
- Expected payment table
- Names/nicknames
- Matching rules
Outputs:
- Paid / unpaid list
- Amount expected vs received
- Unmatched transactions
- Polite reminder draft

## Slide 12 — Sample setup: meal planning helper
Use case: reduce weekly decision fatigue
Project context:
- Family preferences
- Allergies / dislikes
- Budget
- Usual shops
Outputs:
- Meal plan
- Grocery list by section
- Prep plan
- Leftovers plan

## Slide 13 — How to set up a Project/Gem
- Create a new Claude Project / ChatGPT Project / Gemini Gem
- Give it a clear job title
- Paste your context and rules
- Add examples of good outputs
- Test with real messy input
- Refine the instructions

## Slide 14 — Starter instruction template
You are my assistant for [task].
Context:
- [Who/what this is for]
- [Important recurring details]
Rules:
- [What to always do]
- [What to never do]
Output format:
- [Table/checklist/message/etc.]
Before final answer:
- Flag missing info or assumptions.
- Ask before doing anything irreversible.

## Slide 15 — Hands-on activity
1. Pick one recurring task
2. Fill in the starter template
3. Paste one messy real example
4. Ask the AI for a structured output
5. Improve the instructions once
6. Share one thing that worked / one thing that failed

## Slide 16 — Optional advanced path
Projects/Gems are enough for many workflows.
Advanced assistants can connect to:
- Google Sheets
- Calendar
- Telegram/WhatsApp
- email
- files
But this requires technical setup, permissions, and more safeguards.

## Slide 17 — Takeaway
You are not trying to replace yourself.
You are capturing the boring repeatable parts so you can spend less attention on admin.
Start small. Keep human approval. Improve weekly.
